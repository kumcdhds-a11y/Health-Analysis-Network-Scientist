Methods

We analyzed Korea National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey participants using complex-survey methods accounting for stratification, clustering, and sampling weights. Smoking and alcohol were grouped as never versus past or current. Body fat, %, Appendicular skeletal muscle, %, Body mass index, kg/m2 served as exposures and Hepatic steatosis, Diabetes mellitus as outcomes, adjusting for Age, years, Male sex, Ever smoking (past/current), Ever alcohol (past/current). Continuous exposures were modeled per 1-SD. Binary outcomes used survey-weighted logistic regression, and continuous outcomes used survey-weighted linear regression. Multiplicity was controlled with the Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate.

Results

Body mass index, kg/m2 was positively associated with Hepatic steatosis (odds ratio 3.41, 95% CI 3.07 to 3.79, FDR q 0.000). Appendicular skeletal muscle, % was inversely associated with Hepatic steatosis (odds ratio 0.27, 95% CI 0.23 to 0.31, FDR q 0.000). Body fat, % was positively associated with Hepatic steatosis (odds ratio 3.54, 95% CI 3.11 to 4.03, FDR q 0.000). Body mass index, kg/m2 was positively associated with Diabetes mellitus (odds ratio 1.63, 95% CI 1.49 to 1.78, FDR q 0.000). Appendicular skeletal muscle, % was inversely associated with Diabetes mellitus (odds ratio 0.53, 95% CI 0.45 to 0.64, FDR q 0.000). Body fat, % was positively associated with Diabetes mellitus (odds ratio 1.40, 95% CI 1.17 to 1.68, FDR q 0.000).

Discussion

These survey-weighted associations are hypothesis-generating and require external validation. The cross-sectional design precludes causal inference.
